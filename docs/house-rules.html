House Rules

555 McLean Ave — a resident-owned cooperative in Yonkers, NY.

These House Rules apply to all shareholders, their household members, guests, subtenants, and anyone acting on their behalf. They are adopted by the Board of Directors under the authority of the Proprietary Lease and may be amended by the Board at any time. Where these rules and the Proprietary Lease conflict, the Lease controls.

1.Noise & Quiet Enjoyment

  • Acceptable hours for noise are 8:00 AM to 10:00 PM; quiet hours run from 10:00 PM to 8:00 AM daily. No construction or renovation noise on Sundays or legal holidays.
  • No resident shall make or permit noise that unreasonably disturbs neighbors at any hour (loud music, TVs, instruments, parties, barking, etc.).
  • At least 80% of the floor area of each apartment (excluding kitchens and bathrooms) must be covered with rugs or carpeting to reduce sound transmission.

2.Common Areas, Hallways & Lobbies

  • Hallways, stairwells, lobbies, and landings must be kept clear at all times. Do not store shoes, mats, strollers, bicycles, furniture, or personal items in these areas — they are fire-egress paths.
  • Do not place mats, signs, decorations, or shoe racks outside apartment doors.
  • Bicycles, scooters, and carts may not be ridden in common areas and must be stored inside apartments or in designated storage only.
  • Children may not play in hallways, stairwells, elevators, the lobby, or the parking areas.

3.Move-In / Move-Out

  • All moves must be scheduled in advance with the managing agent.
  • Permitted moving hours: 9:00 AM – 5:00 PM, Monday–Saturday. No moves on Sundays or legal holidays.
  • A refundable move deposit of $500 is required to cover any damage to common areas; the elevator must be padded for the move.
  • See the Move-In / Move-Out Policy for full details.

4.Alterations & Renovations

  • No structural, plumbing, electrical, flooring, or wall alterations may begin until a signed Alteration Agreement is approved by the Board.
  • Contractors must provide proof of license and insurance naming the co-op as additional insured before work starts.
  • Permitted construction hours: 9:00 AM – 5:00 PM, Monday–Friday (no weekends or holidays). No work that shuts off building water, gas, or electricity without prior written approval and notice to affected residents.

5.Pets

  • A maximum of two pets per apartment, each registered with the managing agent; no pet over 50 lbs and no breeds prohibited by the co-op's insurer.
  • All pets must be leashed in common areas and never left unattended there.
  • Owners must clean up after pets immediately, indoors and out.
  • No pet may create a nuisance through noise, odor, or aggression.

6.Garbage, Recycling & Sanitation

  • Household trash must be bagged, tied, and placed in designated containers or the chute only. Break down boxes and follow Yonkers recycling rules (paper/cardboard, metal/glass/plastic separated).
  • No bulk items, furniture, mattresses, electronics, or construction debris in the regular trash — arrange bulk pickup through the managing agent.
  • Do not leave trash in hallways, stairwells, or outside apartment doors.

7.Parking & Vehicles

  • Parking spaces are assigned through the parking waitlist; spaces may not be sublet or transferred without Board approval.
  • 5 MPH speed limit in all lots and driveways; watch for children.
  • No vehicle repairs, washing, or storage of inoperable or unregistered vehicles on the property. No blocking driveways, fire lanes, dumpsters, or other spaces.

8.Laundry Room

  • The laundry room is located on the M floor and is open 24 hours a day. Be considerate of neighbors and observe quiet hours (no slamming machines or doors between 10:00 PM and 8:00 AM).
  • Payment: add cash to your laundry card, or pay through the MyMagic+ PASS app (download for iPhone).
  • Remove laundry promptly; do not leave clothing unattended for long periods.
  • Return the laundry cart promptly so other residents can use it.
  • Clean lint traps after each use; report broken machines to the managing agent.

9.Packages & Deliveries

  • Retrieve packages promptly so lobbies and common areas stay clear. The building is not responsible for lost or stolen packages.
  • Large-item and furniture deliveries should be scheduled like a move — see the Move-In / Move-Out Policy.

10.Occupancy, Guests & Subletting

  • Apartments are for residential use only by the shareholder and the persons named in the proprietary lease / stock certificate.
  • Short-term rentals (Airbnb, VRBO, and similar) are prohibited.
  • Subletting requires Board approval and a completed Sublet Application; subtenants are bound by these House Rules.
  • Guests staying longer than 30 consecutive days must be disclosed to the Board.

11.Safety & Security

  • Do not prop open or disable building entrance, lobby, or stairwell doors.
  • Do not admit ("buzz in") people you do not know.
  • Report broken locks, lights, or security concerns to the managing agent promptly. For fire, gas, flooding, or other emergencies, call the building emergency line and 911 immediately.
  • Smoke and carbon monoxide detectors must be kept operational; do not disable them.

12.Windows & Building Exterior

  • Window guards must be installed where required by law for units in which children under 11 reside.
  • Nothing may be hung, shaken, or dried from windows, railings, or fire escapes.
  • No signs, awnings, antennas, satellite dishes, air conditioners, or other fixtures on the exterior or in windows without Board approval. Window A/C units must be installed safely and per any building standard.

13.Smoking

  • Smoking is prohibited in all indoor common areas — lobbies, hallways, stairwells, elevators, laundry — and within 25 feet of building entrances. Smoke must not migrate from an apartment so as to disturb neighbors. Cannabis follows the same policy as tobacco.

14.Plumbing & Maintenance Responsibility

  • Do not put grease, wipes, diapers, or non-flushable items down drains or toilets.
  • Report leaks immediately to limit damage. Shareholders are responsible for damage caused by their own fixtures, appliances, or neglect.
  • Provide reasonable access for building staff to make repairs or inspections with appropriate notice (immediate access in emergencies).

15.Insurance (HO-6) — Required

  • Every shareholder must maintain an in-force HO-6 unit-owner / co-op policy at all times. See the Insurance Requirements for minimum coverage amounts and the additional-insured requirements.
  • Subtenants must carry HO-4 renter's coverage.

16.Enforcement & Fines

  • Violations may result in written notice, fines, and — for repeated or serious violations — action under the Proprietary Lease.
  • Fine schedule: first notice — written warning; second — $100; each subsequent occurrence — $250. Shareholders are responsible for violations by their household members, guests, and subtenants.
  • Fines and unreimbursed costs may be added to maintenance as additional rent.
Adopted by the Board of Directors of 555 McLean Ave. Questions? Contact the Board.
