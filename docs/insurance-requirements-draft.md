# 555 McLean Ave — Insurance Requirements (DRAFT for Board Adoption)

> **Status: DRAFT.** This is a template for the Board to confirm and publish (as
> `insurance-requirements.pdf` per `docs/README.md`). The dollar amounts below
> are common co-op minimums — **confirm them against the Proprietary Lease and
> with the co-op's insurance broker before adopting**, since the Lease may
> already set a required amount.

## Who must be insured
- **Every shareholder** must maintain a current **HO-6** policy (the unit-owner /
  co-op apartment policy) for as long as they own shares.
- **Every subtenant** must maintain an **HO-4** renter's policy.
- Proof of coverage is required **at closing and at every annual renewal.**

## Minimum required coverage

| Coverage | Recommended minimum |
|---|---|
| **Personal liability** | **$300,000** (the Board may require **$500,000**) |
| **Dwelling / betterments & improvements** (Coverage A) | Enough to repair the unit's interior, fixtures, flooring, and any improvements — confirm a figure with your broker |
| **Loss of use / additional living expense** | Included per standard HO-6 |
| **Loss assessment** | **$50,000** recommended (covers your share of a building loss assessed to shareholders) |
| **Water damage / sewer & drain backup** | Included — strongly recommended endorsement |
| **Deductible** | $1,000 or less recommended |

> The single number most boards enforce is **personal liability of at least
> $300,000**. Many co-ops require **$500,000**. Pick one and state it plainly.

## Required endorsements / naming
The policy (and the Certificate of Insurance) must:
- Name the cooperative — **555 McLean Ave Owners Corp. [confirm exact legal
  name]** — as **additional insured**.
- Name the **managing agent** as additional insured / certificate holder
  (confirm name).
- List the co-op and managing agent as **certificate holders** so they receive
  notice of cancellation or non-renewal.

## Proof of insurance
- Submit a **Certificate of Insurance (COI)** showing the above to the managing
  agent **before closing** and again **at each renewal**.
- A shareholder whose coverage lapses may be required to obtain
  Board-force-placed coverage at the shareholder's expense, and a lapse is a
  violation of the House Rules and Proprietary Lease.

## Why HO-6 matters
The co-op's master policy covers the building structure and common areas — it
does **not** cover the interior of your apartment, your belongings, your
liability, or upgrades you made. An HO-6 policy fills that gap and protects you
(and your neighbors) if, for example, a leak from your unit damages the
apartment below.
