# 555 McLean Ave — Insurance Requirements

> Adopted figures below reflect the Board's recommended minimums. Where the
> Proprietary Lease sets a higher requirement, the Lease controls. Publish as
> `insurance-requirements.pdf` per `docs/README.md`.

## Who must be insured
- **Every shareholder** must maintain a current **HO-6** policy (the unit-owner /
  co-op apartment policy) for as long as they own shares.
- **Every subtenant** must maintain an **HO-4** renter's policy.
- Proof of coverage is required **at closing and at every annual renewal.**

## Minimum required coverage

| Coverage | Required minimum |
|---|---|
| **Personal liability** | **$300,000** |
| **Dwelling / betterments & improvements** (Coverage A) | Enough to fully repair the unit's interior, fixtures, flooring, and improvements |
| **Loss of use / additional living expense** | Included per standard HO-6 |
| **Loss assessment** | **$50,000** |
| **Water damage / sewer & drain backup** | Included (endorsement required) |
| **Deductible** | **$1,000 or less** |

> The single enforced number is **personal liability of at least $300,000.**

## Required endorsements / naming
The policy and the Certificate of Insurance must:
- Name the cooperative — **555 McLean Ave Owners Corp. [confirm exact legal
  name]** — as **additional insured**.
- Name the **managing agent** as additional insured / certificate holder.
- List the co-op and managing agent as **certificate holders** so they receive
  notice of cancellation or non-renewal.

## Proof of insurance
- Submit a **Certificate of Insurance (COI)** showing the above to the managing
  agent **before closing** and again **at each renewal**.
- If coverage lapses, the Board may obtain force-placed coverage at the
  shareholder's expense; a lapse is a violation of the House Rules and
  Proprietary Lease.

## Why HO-6 matters
The co-op's master policy covers the building structure and common areas — it
does **not** cover the interior of your apartment, your belongings, your
liability, or upgrades you made. An HO-6 policy fills that gap and protects you
(and your neighbors) if, for example, a leak from your unit damages the
apartment below.
